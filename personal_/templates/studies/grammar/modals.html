What are modal verbs?

They are special verbs that behave irregularly in English. They are different from normal verbs. They give additional information about the function of the main verb that follows it.

Modal Verb Expressing Example
must, have to, have got to Strong obligation You must stop when the traffic lights turn red.
logical conclusion / Certainty He must be very tired. He's been working all day long.
must not prohibition You must not smoke in the hospital.
have not lack of necessity I can hear you, you don't have to shout.
can ability I can swim.
permission Can I use your phone, please?
possibility Smoking can cause cancer.
could ability in the past When I was younger I could run fast.
polite permission Excuse me, could I just say something?
possibility It could rain tomorrow!
may polite permission May I use your phone, please?
possibility, probability It may rain tomorrow!
might polite permission Might I suggest an idea?
possibility, probability I might go on holiday to Australia next year.
need not lack of necessity/absence of obligation I need not buy tomatoes. There are plenty of tomatoes in the fridge.
should/ought to 50 % obligation I should / ought to see a doctor. I have a terrible headache.
advice You should / ought to revise your lessons
logical conclusion He should / ought to be very tired. He's been working all day long.
had better, should, ought to advice You'd better revise your lessons
should have, should not have regret [not] doing an action I had a test this morning, I should have studied
be supposed to Expectation The game is supposed to bigin at 10:00
let's, why not, sall [we/I] Making suggestion
Degrees of Certainty : Present time modals Expression
95% Must He must be sick
less than 50% May be, might be, could be He might be sick
Degrees of Certainty : Present time Negative modals Expression
95% Must not, could not, can not He can't be sick
less than 50% May not be, might not be He might not be sick
Degrees of Certainty : Past time modals Expression
95% Mast have been He must have been sick
less than 50% [May, might, could] have been He might have been be sick
Degrees of Certainty : future time modals Expression
95% [should, ought to] do He should do well
less than 50% [May, might, could] do He might do well
