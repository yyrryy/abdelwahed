- Simple tenses:

  • Simple prensent:

    Used to express events/situation that are exist always, usually abitually
    Ex: - it Snows in alaska - I watch television everyday (Abitually)

  • Simple past:

    Expresses events that bigen and end in the past
    Ex: it snowed yesterday

  • Simple future

    Expresses events that bigen and end in the future
    Ex: it will snow next week

- Progressive tenses

  • Present continiuos:

    For action that began in the past, in progress now and probably will continue
    Ex: Tom is sleeping right now (the action started in the past, happening right now, and my continued in the future)

  • Past continiuos:

    For action that began in the past, in progress in a particullar moment in the past and probably continued after that particullar moment
    Ex: Tom was sleeping when I arrived. (the sleep started before I arrived, happening when I arrived, and probably continiued after I arrived)

  • Future continiuos:

    For action that will begin in the future, in progress in a particullar moment in the future and will probably after that particullar moment
    Ex: Tom well be sleeping when we arrive (the action will start in the fututre, happening when I will arrive, and probably will continue after I arrived)
